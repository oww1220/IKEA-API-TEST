Falling asleep As we’ve established, a darker bedroom is crucial for a good night’s sleep. To prepare your body, aim for a gradually darkening environment at home.
Dimming lights through a dimmer switch on overhead lights or side lamps a full hour before bedtime will notify your brain that it should begin the physiological processes required for sleep.

Staying asleep In order to maintain good sleep, make sure all lights are switched off and curtains/blinds are closed. Use heavy or blackout curtains which block out any lights coming from the street. An eye mask can also help ensure that you are protected against light.

Humidity is an important air quality factor to take into consideration. Too much humidity prevents moisture evaporating from our body, making us feel uncomfortably hot and sticky. Dampness also makes things worse for sleep due to the fact that it causes congestion, as high moisture contributes to growth of molds and allergens, which may affect your sleep.

You should try to minimize the impact of indoor pollutants.
The easiest way to do this is by keeping a window open and ensuring that fresh air is circulating or by adding Plants, especially spider plants and Aloe Vera are considered to be the best natural air purifiers.

These colours have been found to elicit feelings of relaxation which in turn help you fall asleep faster, and sleep better.
